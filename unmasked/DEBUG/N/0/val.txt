In mathematics , Puiseux series are a generalization of power series , first introduced by Isaac Newton in 1676 and rediscovered by Victor Puiseux in 1850 , that allows for negative and fractional exponents of the indeterminate T. A Puiseux series in the indeterminate T is a Laurent series in T1/n , where n is a positive integer .
